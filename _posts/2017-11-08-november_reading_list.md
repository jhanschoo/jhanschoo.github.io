I'm looking for people interested in reading, and forming a reading
group for the following books on which I am at various levels of
progress:

* Rudin, Walter, *Principles of Mathematical Analysis, 3e*.
  * Offers an introduction to analysis.
* Hoffman and Kunze, *Linear Algebra, 2e*
  * Offers an introduction to linear algrbra. I am almost done with
    this book.
* Munkres, *Topology, 2e*
  * Offers an introduction to topology.
* Kallenberg, Olav, *Foundations of Modern Probability, 2e*
  * Surveys probability theory.
* Awodey *Category Theory, 2e*
  * Introduces category theory.
* Bertot and Castéran, *Interactive Theorem Proving and Program Development*
  * Introduction to languages with dependent types and formal
    verification in Coq.
* Cover, Thomas, *Elements of Information Theory, 2e*
  * Introduction to Information Theory.
